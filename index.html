About

I am a fourth-year PhD student at the Decision Making group of University of Tübingen, advised by Prof. Setareh Maghsudi. My research focuses on online decision-making problems under uncertainty. I develop algorithms and frameworks to model and solve decision-making problems with various feedback models and objectives. In particular, my research addresses bandit problems where

  • environment is changing,
  • environment is structured,
  • observations are costly,
  • features are costly,
  • features are high-dimensional,
  • feedback is delayed.

I am interested in the math behind the learning systems. That is why, in my research, I focus on theoretical aspects of the online learning problems. In addition, together with my collaborators, we have applied the developed algorithms to build online recommender systems, analyze the development of Covid-19, model computation offloading of users' mobile devices to edge servers, and design medical decision-support systems. In addition to my research, I have also been consulting on algorithm development for real-time task planning in changing environments. Currently, I am doing research on forward and inverse reinforcement learning problems in multi-agent setting to design learning methods for explaining sequential decision-making on the basis of demonstrated behavior by a sub-optimal learner.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my master studies in Mathematical Modelling within an Erasmus Mundus international joint master program at University of Hamburg and obtained a bachelor’s degree in Pure Mathematics from Iran University of Science and Technology.
